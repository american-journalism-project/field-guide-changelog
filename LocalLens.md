# LocalLens

## Details
- URL: thelocallens.org
- Highlight: An AI-powered public meeting database that provides searchable summaries of local government meetings across the United States.
- Summary: LocalLens is a "metal detector" for local government activity. It uses AI to quickly summarize meetings and publish that information in a well-designed, simplified format.

## What is this for?
LocalLens publishes AI summaries of official meetings with the intent to provide a fast and easy way for journalists and engaged citizens to learn about what took place in each meeting. While the AI summaries have a high level of accuracy, they are each intended to be verified before quoting or publishing.

The scale of meetings that LocalLens can cover and transcribe allows it to be a useful tool for both understanding how a government entity operates within a specific region, or how a single issue or topic is being discussed across many different regions in the United States.

### How is AI used?
AI is used throughout the entire process for summarizing meetings. Their technology blends a variety of LLMs and post-processing checks in an attempt to make their AI written summaries meet the editorial standards of a newsroom. There is no human-in-the-loop activity in its output. In some cases, AI is also used in the video collection process to help ingest civic meeting recordings from sites that are not hosted on a common CMS platform.

### What makes it different from other tools?
LocalLens is a mission-driven, technology organization that probably has the greatest ability to quickly scale out nationally, because it relies almost entirely on machines to process and summarize meetings. Their stated goal is to empower journalists, and not replace them. Where most others that do automated meeting summaries have some level of human-assisted or human-in-the-loop oversight, LocalLens clearly states "Every meeting summary you read on this website is written by a computer."

### Pros
- Meetings are automatically processed nightly
- Their software can transcribe and summarize almost any kind of local meeting recording

### Cons
- No human in the loop review of content, so verification of accuracy is required
