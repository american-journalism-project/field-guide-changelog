# Everlaw

## Details
- URL: everlaw.com
- Highlight: Project-based document assistant made for large investigations.
- Summary: Everlaw is primarily designed for legal teams needing software to assist with litigation discovery, but it has potential to be effective for investigative work.

## What is this for?
Everlaw’s core strength is its ability to unify a large set of documents, providing an all-in-one platform for an individual or team to explore. For journalists, this functionality is ideal for supporting big-story investigative work. The software’s claimed excellence in advanced optical text recognition (OCR) makes it particularly useful for projects that require extracting text from poorly formatted PDFs or images.

Other platform features include advanced search tools to query across all documents, a document analytics and data visualizer, and the ability to run advanced clustering tasks, such as word clouds to identify trends.

### How is AI used?
Everlaw AI Assistant uses generative AI to drive “faster document review with near-instant document insights, summaries, and the ability to answer open-ended questions with direct citations to supporting evidence for easy verification,” according to information on their website. AI is also used across some of the core features like: document clustering, categorizing, suggestions, summarization, and entity extraction.

### What makes it different from other tools?
Where many other AI tools built for newsrooms focus on using the power and speed of cheap transcription and summarization to help uncover what’s is happening across a wide spectrum of meetings and events, Everlaw is meant to help investigators focus and search more deeply on a limited topic and set of documents that are uploaded into projects, with visibility and admin controls.

### Pros
- Offers fast product support from real-life humans that work at Everlaw
- An all-in-one, feature-rich, project-based focus makes it different from many other tools available to newsrooms

### Cons
- No open trial. Interested newsrooms are required to submit a form in order to be accepted into their Everlaw For Good program.
