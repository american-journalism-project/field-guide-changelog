# Hamlet

## Details
- URL: myhamlet.com
- Highlight: Hamlet’s AI-powered platform is backed by human verification to provide automated summaries with historical, searchable meeting data from hundreds of U.S. jurisdictions across multiple industries including real estate, renewable energy and government administration.
- Summary: Hamlet is a for-profit software service that synthesizes city council and planning commission meeting data primarily for real estate developers. A version of the product will soon be available for community reporting.

## What is this for?
The core features of Hamlet that have potential utility for newsrooms are:
- Full text search capabilities of across all public meetings it has transcribed
- Additional “sentiment” and “pattern” searching across each meeting’s archive
- An agenda monitoring tool

### How is AI used?
Hamlet uses “off the shelf tools” for their transcription services but have augmented the speaker identification step with third party source matching to greatly improve accuracy. Public comments are not matched to identify speakers. Hamlet offers a separate product for governments that uses more human-in-the-loop verification, but for basic summarization tasks, AI is entirely used to transcribe and summarize. Hamlet has recently previewed a new feature that uses multi-modal analysis to detect and categorize sentiment in public meetings - meaning the AI is looking, for example, at who is angry or happy when they are talking.

### What makes it different from other tools?
Hamlet goes beyond basic transcription and summarization of meeting videos. It also synthesizes and aggregates other relevant data like voting history of commissioners to provide additional context to their project data.

Hamlet also offers the capability to store and search through multiple years of meeting data. Their full text search capabilities appear to be ahead of the class, in terms of accuracy and the volume of indexed data they are able to query across.

Hamlet was born out of the frustration of watching city council meetings. It has a client base beyond the news media industry, but is motivated to offer a version of the product to journalists.

### Pros
Unrealized potential for newsrooms to help piece together multiple data sources
Full text search of many years of meeting data could be a unique offering

### Cons
Still emerging as a new newsroom product
List of cities covered is currently concentrated in areas where there are more active real estate projects
