# City Meetings NYC 

## Details
- URL: citymeetings.nyc
- Highlight: Human directed, AI-powered public meeting summaries for New York City council meetings. 
- Summary: CityMeetings NYC combines AI and human editorial judgement to produce meeting summaries and transcriptions for New York City Council meetings. The website states, “It's the easiest way to find and share the 3-minute Q&A or testimony you care about in a 12-hour hearing.”	

## What is this for?
There are two versions of citymeetings.nyc, but the output for both is about the same. The free version aims to be a direct to the public, primary news source for “engaged citizens and professionals.

The site’s founder, Vikram Oberoi, created citymeetings.nyc to make an easier to navigate version of the city's official website and source of City Council meetings. We mention him here because as the founder and de facto editor-in-chief, Oberoi is hyperfocused on serving this specific market with no public plans to scale.

On citymeetings.nyc, each meeting has its own landing page that includes a summary and key events in each meeting are segmented into chapters, so users can link straight into a specific moment of the meeting. Each of these chapters includes a summary, transcript, and a video of that specific segment with timestamps to help navigate.

The Pro version is meant for newsrooms, as well as government officials, policymakers, and other civic professionals. It features expanded coverage of meetings (full list), near-instant transcription, and more advanced “trustworthy & powerful AI tools.

### How is AI used?
Site founder Oberoi uses "human-AI teaming.” More from Oberoi: “I don't rely on the AI to create the output entirely. It's another thing that we can talk about more, but I don't believe that is a great way to use AI today. I also don't think it's going to be a great way to use AI in the future, even if you assume amazing developments in AI technology; if you just use AI alone, there's human judgment and your desires and goals that you didn't fuse into that process. citymeetings is the product of me, and there's a lot of preferences that go into what I create there.

### What makes it different from other tools?
Citymeetings is a founder-led project from Vikram Oberoi. Both his technical skills and editorial judgement sit at the forefront of each meeting's summary and make citymeetings.nyc a primary source, journalistic endeavor with Oberoi as its de facto editor in chief.

Oberoi has stated that his aim is "for citymeetings.nyc to be the most comprehensive and authoritative source on public proceedings in New York City." Oberoi is also a collaborative and experienced technology leader in this evolving community of tools and products.

### Pros
- Meeting summaries are available both on the website and can be delivered by email.  
- Each meeting is broken up into segments that have their own landing page, summary information, and accompanying source video. The raw transcript is also available on these segment pages. 
- The summaries have all been made with human oversight, and since that human is always the same person (Oberoi), consistency and context between meetings appears to be a strength.

### Cons
- It covers New York only, and mostly just New York City Council meetings.
