# Gigafact Parser

## Details
- URL: gigafact.org/parser/
- Highlight: A civic platform for news professionals to transcribe and analyze audio and video recordings of public comments by public officials to surface their claims and talking points. 
- Summary: Gigafact Parser aids journalists in monitoring and synthesizing comments made by public figures. It supports fact-checking, post-debate analysis, voter guides and profiles of politicians	

## What is this for?
Parser’s primary function is to bring together, into a searchable and indexed dataset, the public statements of public figures. The ideal use case is to collect statements made in unofficial settings such as in TV, radio or podcast appearances.

Using Parser to help cover political campaigns is the most obvious application of its tools. Parser performs best in any scenario where candidates or public officials are making public comments about a variety of topics and in a variety of places. Parser is built to bring all those comments together to allow a reporter to research the claims that are made.

### How is AI used?
Parser uses AI transcription tools to parse through large recordings and break up the conversations into subjects and claims that are attributed to the speaker. Those segmented claims are then separated out of the larger conversation by the Parser AI and connected to similar claims made by that person on the same topic(s). The dataset is built around the AI identifying and indexing these topic-based claims and statements.

### What makes it different from other tools?
Parser is unique in its ability to group together publicly-made comments and filter them by topics. This provides a tool that fits a very specific need. Parser’s value is tied to its growing dataset of public comments. While most other tools in this space work with only official meetings and data, Parser can handle a subset of official meetings but is built more to analyze interview recordings.

### Pros
- Parser can be a time-saving tool when used for understanding what was said by an individual in a public appearance and when tracking how their statements may have changed over time.
- Parser’s database of statements made by public officials is unique and offers journalists an easy way to connect multiple comments made over time on similar topics (most services for now only transcribe and summarize single meeting events and cannot easily connect them together)

### Cons
- Though Parser is used for tracking comments of public figures, it is not as useful for tracking official public gatherings, like city council meetings, where there are a large number of speakers and where the general public has an opportunity to make comments. Parser tries to assign all statements made in a recording to an individual profile, so i is better intended use is when all of the speakers in a recording are public figures. In situations where there is a lot of cross-talk, Parser is not an ideal tool.
- The data Parser collects is mostly public to all, meaning source recordings that are uploaded to Parser become a part of the public dataset for that speaker’s public profile on the site. There is an ability to upload a private recording, but other services are likely better suited for this private feature.
